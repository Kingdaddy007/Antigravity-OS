# Seedance Copyright and Likeness Safety

## Scope

This skill is a risk-screening workflow, not legal advice and not a promise that a platform filter will allow or reject content.

## Verified Platform Rule

ByteDance's 2026-02-12 official Seedance 2.0 launch states that real-person portrait references require identity verification or prior legal authorization. This was rechecked on 2026-07-13. Discover and load the `source-verification` baseline through the active manifest rather than assuming a host-specific relative path.

## Workflow

1. Inventory every reference asset, named person, character, brand, logo, voice, and source clip.
2. Record ownership, license, consent, intended audience, territory, commercial use, and publication channel.
3. Separate platform-policy uncertainty from legal-rights uncertainty; neither substitutes for the other.
4. Prefer original or licensed assets and descriptive creative direction over imitation of protected characters, living artists, celebrity likenesses, or branded campaigns.
5. For real-person likeness or voice, require documented consent and any platform identity-verification step before upload.
6. For ambiguous commercial use, pause publication and request qualified review from the rights owner or appropriate legal authority.
7. Obtain explicit approval before uploading third-party assets or publishing generated output.

## Output

Report:

- assets and rights basis;
- unresolved copyright, trademark, privacy, publicity, or consent questions;
- safer substitutions;
- platform checks still required;
- publication decision: proceed, revise, or hold.
