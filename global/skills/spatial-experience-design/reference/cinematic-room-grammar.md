# Cinematic Room Grammar

Use this when writing image or video prompts for interior, spatial, gallery, decor, showroom, or portfolio scenes. The room is rarely boring; the camera logic is usually under-authored.

## Core Rule

Do not prompt a beautiful room. Prompt a way of seeing the room.

Every room asset must define:

- Desired visitor belief.
- Taste world.
- One dominant light source.
- Camera side: shadow-side, highlight-side, threshold-side, detail-side, or POV.
- What is withheld at the start.
- What detail carries intimacy or proof.
- Foreground, midground, and background roles.
- Text-safe zone.
- Forbidden cliches.

## Light

- Use one dominant light source before adding complexity.
- Use shadow-side framing for drama, intimacy, and premium restraint.
- Use highlight-side framing only when clarity, openness, or hospitality warmth is the point.
- Let light reveal material before it reveals the full room.
- Treat lamp glow, morning light, doorway spill, and gallery spotlight as narrative agents.

## Framing

- Do not show the whole room too early.
- Break a room into details: linen edge, stone vein, wood grain, plaster texture, bronze handle, art shadow, chair silhouette, curtain movement.
- Use close detail when the brand needs material intelligence.
- Use threshold framing when the brand needs invitation or exclusivity.
- Use lateral framing when the brand needs gallery-like curation.
- Use POV framing when the visitor should feel inside the space.

## Depth

Build every cinematic room as a depth sandwich:

```text
Foreground: blur, veil, curtain, object edge, hand, plant shadow, glass edge
Midground: subject, seating moment, table, fixture, doorway, material plane
Background: room atmosphere, plaster wall, art, corridor, light source
```

Use focus shift to create curiosity: blurred foreground -> designed object -> room reveal.

## Camera Movement

| Movement | Use For | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Slow push-in | Desire, intimacy, tension | Come closer; notice atmosphere |
| Lateral glide | Gallery discipline, curation | This is composed |
| Detail-to-room pullback | Material intelligence | The detail belongs to the whole |
| Threshold move | Private-world invitation | You are being allowed in |
| Locked frame with moving light | Restraint, quiet luxury | The room is alive without spectacle |
| POV move | Embodiment | The visitor is inside the space |

## Human Trace

Use human presence as evidence, not stock lifestyle:

- chair slightly pulled out
- book open
- curtain touched
- table set
- cup steam
- lamp switched on
- hand selecting fabric

Avoid smiling models, generic cooking, staged laughter, and lifestyle-blogger dominance unless the brand specifically needs it.

## Prompt Quality Gate

Reject prompts that:

- say only "luxury", "modern", "elegant", or "cinematic"
- reveal the full room immediately when suspense is needed
- lack light direction
- lack camera position
- lack foreground/midground/background
- lack text-safe zone
- could be used for any interior designer without change
