# Compression Cascade Syntax

**Source:** Blindness • José Saramago • 2008 • Dystopian Literary Fiction
**Motion personality:** Jittery
**Density:** Maximalist
**Psychological lever:** Dread anticipation + loss aversion + physiological discomfort

---

## How It Works

Eliminate breathing room. Compress time, increase density, refuse to give the
user room to process. The style mirrors the content — claustrophobia through form.

**In the book:** Prose eliminates paragraph breaks and quotation marks. Dialogue
runs into narration into thought. Each chapter compresses time and increases
character density. Panic accelerates because prose refuses breathing room.

**In web design:** Dense text blocks, shrinking padding, accelerating scroll.
The discomfort IS the experience.

---

## Web Translation

**Hero / above-the-fold:**
- Dense text block — no paragraph breaks, smaller font (14–16px), tight line-height (1.3–1.4)
- Headline: "It happened to one then another then everyone"
- No punctuation in headline, run-on sentence structure
- Scroll accelerates automatically (Lenis with velocity multiplier)

**Spatial / 3D / canvas:**
- R3F particle system — thousands of small white spheres representing people
- Camera starts distant, dollies in rapidly as particles turn white (blindness spreading)
- Instanced meshes for performance
- useFrame drives particle color shift based on contagion algorithm (neighbor proximity)

**Scroll pacing:**
- 1.5x viewport heights per chapter equivalent (fast)
- Linear easing (no ease, constant velocity, uncomfortable)
- Micro-animation: text containers shrink padding (24px → 8px) as scroll progresses
- Compresses breathing room

**Micro-copy & CTA:**
- CTA appears mid-crisis (30% scroll), not resolution point
- Example: "See what happens next" — bottom-left, overlapping text, low contrast (hard to read — intentional)
