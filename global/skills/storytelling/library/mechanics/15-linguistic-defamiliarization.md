# Linguistic De-familiarization

**Source:** Annihilation • Jeff VanderMeer • 2014 • Weird Fiction
**Motion personality:** Glacial
**Density:** Minimal
**Psychological lever:** Dread anticipation + pattern violation

---

## How It Works

Strip away familiar naming conventions. Refer to things by wrong names. The
absence of identity heightens the sense of an alien, threatening environment.
The dread is in the language itself.

**In the book:** Narrator refers to a biological structure as a "tower" while
others call it a "tunnel." Characters have no names — only roles (Biologist,
Anthropologist). Identity is stripped, heightening alien-ness.

**In web design:** Use naming conventions and visual metaphors that create subtle
rifts in perception. The familiar becomes unfamiliar.

---

## Web Translation

**Hero / above-the-fold:**
- Immersive video background
- Headline: "The tower was a tunnel."
- The true nature of the environment is withheld

**Spatial / 3D / canvas:**
- Slow, drifting camera through dense, organic 3D environment
- R3F Points or Cloud components
- Environment shifts subtly as user explores

**Scroll pacing:**
- High scroll-distance-to-event ratio
- power4.inOut easing
- "Creeping" growth animation on text — letters slowly expand or shift

**Micro-copy & CTA:**
- Hidden until hover, creating sense of discovery
- Example: "Observe the Specimen"
