# Casual Glossary Immersion

**Source:** Never Let Me Go • Kazuo Ishiguro • 2005 • Dystopian Literary Fiction
**Motion personality:** Glacial
**Density:** Minimal
**Psychological lever:** Information asymmetry + dread anticipation

---

## How It Works

Withhold the monster. Build a world through implication and casual reference
to things the user doesn't understand yet. The absence of explanation IS the hook.

**In the book:** Narrator casually mentions "donations" and "completing" as if
these are normal things. Reader slowly realizes these terms refer to organ
harvesting and death. The world is understood through implication.

**In web design:** Build the world through implication. Don't explain — let the
user discover. The familiar becomes unfamiliar through context.

---

## Web Translation

**Hero / above-the-fold:**
- Full-bleed image of elite boarding school, soft focus
- Headline: "The breakthrough everyone's been waiting for."
- The "breakthrough" is withheld

**Spatial / 3D / canvas:**
- R3F scene — aerial view of Westfield campus
- Single red cross texture flickers on floor plan
- Scene freezes if user tries to inspect — cursor turns to hourglass

**Scroll pacing:**
- 5x viewport heights to the revelation
- power4.inOut easing
- Micro-animation: nav bar text gradually shifts from "Our Services" to "Your Obligations" to "Required Donations"

**Micro-copy & CTA:**
- Positioned at the "turn" — where user realizes the truth
- Example: "Schedule Your Appointment." — clinical, unsettling
