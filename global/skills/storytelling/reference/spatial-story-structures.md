# Spatial Story Structures

Use these as candidate narrative forms when developing the story contribution to `experience-blueprint.md` or an approved equivalent. Select, reorder, hybridize, or reject them according to the creative brief and first-known priority.

## Atmosphere-Led Decorator Portfolio

1. Atmosphere: enter the studio's taste world.
2. Taste: show strongest finished room.
3. Transformation: before/after or problem/resolution.
4. Proof: project facts, client words, press, constraints.
5. Method: how the studio thinks and collaborates.
6. Inquiry: calm next step.

## Material-Led Studio

1. Macro material entry.
2. Room application.
3. Sourcing/craft proof.
4. Transformation through material decision.
5. Method and constraints.
6. Inquiry.

## Gallery / Editorial Studio

1. Quiet threshold.
2. Room-by-room procession.
3. Archive/project index.
4. Press/proof wall.
5. Studio method.
6. Inquiry.

## Before/After Specialist

1. Tension: what the space used to fail at.
2. Reveal: transformation event.
3. Decision captions.
4. Project proof.
5. Method.
6. Inquiry.

## Proof-First Architecture-Adjacent Practice

1. Rigor, credentials, or project facts.
2. Representative project decision.
3. Spatial atmosphere and result.
4. Method and constraints.
5. Broader work.
6. Selective inquiry.

## Point-of-View-Led Studio

1. Conviction or counter-standard.
2. Work that demonstrates it.
3. Implication for the client's space.
4. Proof and process.
5. Fit and inquiry.

No structure is a universal default. Compare at least two plausible forms before selection.

## Copy Beat Rules

- Visual chapters get short lines.
- Proof chapters get precise captions.
- Method chapters get confident clarity.
- Inquiry chapters get calm specificity.
