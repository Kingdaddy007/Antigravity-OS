# Material and Lighting

Choose color from material and light. Do not choose a palette first.

## Material Scripts

## Wood

- Signals warmth, craft, residence, hand-finished restraint.
- Use visible grain, contact shadows, warm lamplight, and low gloss.
- Avoid generic brown gradients.

## Stone

- Signals permanence, cost, weight, and architectural authority.
- Use grazing light, macro texture, cool-to-warm variation, and hard-edged shadow.
- Avoid flat gray slabs without texture.

## Textile

- Signals softness, intimacy, layering, and decorator taste.
- Use folds, weave, translucency, curtain reveals, and low-amplitude ambient movement.
- Avoid perfect flat color blocks.

## Plaster / Limewash

- Signals quiet luxury and hand-applied craft.
- Use uneven surface, subtle shadow, matte finish, and daylight wash.
- Avoid plain beige backgrounds pretending to be plaster.

## Glass / Mirror

- Signals reflection, reveal, polish, and gallery sophistication.
- Use controlled glare, reflections, edge highlights, and occlusion.
- Avoid cheap glassmorphism unless the spatial world supports it.

## Metal

- Signals fixture quality, precision, and object permanence.
- Use specular highlights, edge light, and material contrast with stone/wood/textile.
- Avoid neon chrome unless the brand is intentionally futuristic.

## Light Behaviors

- Daylight: broad, calm, architectural, credible.
- Lamp glow: intimate, warm, residential, evening.
- Gallery spot: selective, ceremonial, object-focused.
- Shadow pass: threshold, reveal, suspense.
- Light spill: doorway/curtain opening, room transition.

## Rule

Every section should name either a material behavior or a light behavior. If neither exists, the section is probably just content layout.
